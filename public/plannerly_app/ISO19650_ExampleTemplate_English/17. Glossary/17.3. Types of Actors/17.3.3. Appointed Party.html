Appointed Party Definition

The Appointed Party is the foundational level of the information delivery hierarchy. These are the practitioners and specialists who create the actual data, models, and documentation that form the Information Model.

Information Production

The "provider of information concerning works, goods or services" (ISO 19650-1, 3.2.3). This role includes any entity generating information, such as a contractor, subcontractor, supplier, or consultant.

Primary Output

  • Task Information Delivery Plan (TIDP)
  • Discipline-specific BIM Models
  • Asset Data (COBie/Parameters)
  • Internal Quality Assurance records

Dual Roles

An Appointed Party may simultaneously act as an Appointing Party on a different information management project (e.g., a Consultant appointing a specialized surveyor).

BIM Manager Note: Ensure all Appointed Parties are aware of the Project Information Standard before they begin production to avoid significant rework during the coordination phase.
