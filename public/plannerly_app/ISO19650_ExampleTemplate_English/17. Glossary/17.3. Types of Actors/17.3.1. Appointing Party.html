Appointing Party Definition

The Appointing Party is the organization leading the project or asset management. In the context of ISO 19650, they are the primary "receiver" of information and the entity that initiates the procurement process by defining the project's requirements.

Key Characteristics

They are the "receiver of information concerning works, goods or services from a Lead Appointed Party" (ISO 19650-1, 3.2.4).

Potential Entities

  • Project Client / Owner
  • Asset Operator / Manager
  • Lead Designer (to sub-consultants)
  • Main Contractor (to trade contractors)

Primary Responsibilities

  • Establishing the EIR
  • Setting Project Milestones
  • Providing Reference Info
  • Final Information Acceptance

BIM Manager Note: An organization can hold different roles simultaneously across different appointments. For example, a Contractor is the Lead Appointed Party to the Client, but acts as the Appointing Party to their Sub-contractors.
