BIM Use: Building Systems Analysis
System Analysis

A process that measures how a building's performance compares to the specified design. This includes monitoring how the mechanical system operates and how much energy a building uses versus the predicted model.

Other aspects of this analysis include ventilated facade studies, lighting analysis, internal and external CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) airflow, and solar analysis.

Potential Value:
  • Ensure building is operating to specified design and sustainable standards (Performance Verification).
  • Identify opportunities to modify system operations to improve performance.
  • Create "what if" scenarios to change different materials or setpoints to show better or worse performance conditions.
Resources Required:
  • Energy Analysis: Software like IESVE or EnergyPlus.
  • CFD Analysis: Software like Autodesk CFD or Ansys.
  • Lighting Analysis: Tools like ElumTools or Dialux.
  • Building Management System (BMS) data logs.
Team Competencies:
  • Ability to understand and manipulate CMMS and building control systems with the Record Model.
  • Ability to understand typical equipment operation and maintenance practices.
  • Ability to manipulate, navigate, and review a 3D Model.
  • Knowledge of building physics and thermodynamics (for analysis interpretation).
