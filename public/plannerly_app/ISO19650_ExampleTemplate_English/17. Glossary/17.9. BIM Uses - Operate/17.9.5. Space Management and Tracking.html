BIM Use: Space Management and Tracking
Space Management

A process in which BIM is utilized to effectively distribute, manage, and track appropriate spaces and related resources within a facility.

A facility building information model allows the facility management team to analyze the existing use of space and effectively apply transition planning management towards any applicable changes. Such applications are particularly useful during a project's renovation where building segments are to remain occupied.

Potential Value:
  • More easily identify and allocate space for appropriate building use (vacancy tracking).
  • Increase the efficiency of transition planning (move management) during renovations.
  • Proficiently track the use of current space and resources (Departmental chargebacks).
  • Assist in planning future space needs for the facility based on utilization data.
Resources Required:
  • Integration: Bi-directional link between 3D Record Model and CAFM/IWMS software.
  • Software: Space management applications (e.g., Archibus, FM:Systems, EMS).
  • Standards: Defined area calculation standards (e.g., BOMA, ANSI).
Team Competencies:
  • Ability to manipulate, navigate, and review the Record Model.
  • Ability to effectively integrate the Record Model with Facility Management applications.
  • Knowledge of FM area standards (Gross vs. Net vs. Rentable).
