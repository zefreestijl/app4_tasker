Post Occupancy Evaluation (POE)

A formal post-occupancy evaluation (POE) of the buildingâ€™s performance should be conducted at the end of Years 1, 2 and 3.

Evaluation Components

The evaluation should include:

  • An end-user satisfaction survey
  • An energy-use survey
  • An assessment of the overall performance of the asset/facility against the agreed outcomes, targets, and applicable benchmarks.

1. Performance Comparison

The Appointing Party's Information Manager will compare actual performance with the required performance and comment on potential improvements for the end-of-year review.

2. Review Workshop

When the annual analysis report is complete, a workshop will be convened with:

  • Operator / Facility Manager
  • Senior representatives of the Design and Construction team
  • Appointing Party's Information Manager
  • End-user representatives

3. Outcomes & Actions

The purpose is to consider recommendations on how to optimize operational performance. The workshop should conclude with agreed actions necessary to achieve alignment with the objectives, outcomes, and targets as closely and as quickly as possible.
